How to read this


              

                What the scanner is doing
                

                  It generates alternative vertical spreads (“routes”) from your current short premium position and ranks them
                  by whether they improve Theta/day and break-even (sometimes by taking on more defined risk).
                

              


              

                Quick workflow
                
    
                  
  • Enter your current spread (short/long strikes) plus S, IV, and DTE.
    • 
                  
  • Leave “Current credit” blank to use theoretical pricing, or enter your actual fill (per share).
    • 
                  
  • Click Scan rescue routes, then sort the table by Score / Δ columns.
    • 
                

              


              

                Plot legend
                
    
                  
  • Teal dot: your current spread.
    • 
                  
  • Blue dots: candidate rescue routes (top ~40).
    • 
                  
  • X axis: break-even (further left is “better” for downside puts).
    • 
                  
  • Y axis: net theta per day (higher is better). Dashed line marks \( \Theta = 0 \) when in range.
    • 
                

              


              

                Table & deltas
                
    
                  
  • Δ BE = candidate BE − current BE (negative is improvement).
    • 
                  
  • Δ Theta/day = candidate − current (positive is improvement).
    • 
                  
  • Δ Risk = candidate risk − current risk (negative is improvement).
    • 
                

              


              

                Notes / caveats
                
    
                  
  • This is a screening tool (greeks/BE are model-based) — verify with live chain + margin.
    • 
                  
  • Routes may increase buying power; use “Cap at risk” and your broker’s BPR to sanity-check.
